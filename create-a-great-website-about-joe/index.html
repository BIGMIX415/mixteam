Joe Mixon

Running back | Oklahoma | NFL veteran

Joe's game has always been built on patience, contact balance, and finishing power. NFL stats credit him with 7,428 rushing yards, 60 rushing touchdowns, 319 receptions, 2,448 receiving yards, and 14 receiving scores through his listed career totals.

  • Second-round draft pick out of Oklahoma in 2017.
  • Helped Cincinnati reach Super Bowl LVI after the 2021 season.
  • Produced 1,016 rushing yards and 12 total touchdowns for Houston in 2024.
  • Recognized as a two-time Pro Bowler by NFL.com.
54

Jerry Mixon

Inside linebacker | Oregon | San Francisco

Jerry brings downhill instincts to the second level. Oregon lists him at 6-foot-2, 240 pounds, a junior from Sacred Heart Cathedral Prep, and a former consensus three-star prospect with top California linebacker marks.

  • Appeared in eight games as a true freshman in 2023.
  • Recorded his first career sack at Stanford.
  • Played in 11 games as a sophomore in 2024.
  • Reported as a 2026 senior returner after a larger 2025 role.

What Makes Them Great

Joe and Jerry show athletic greatness in different ways. Joe's resume is production over years at the highest level. Jerry's is development, range, and a chance to lead a major college defense.

Power After Contact

Joe's running style turns narrow creases into chunk plays, and his receiving production shows he can stay on the field in multiple game scripts.

Middle-Field Command

Jerry's frame and inside-linebacker role put him at the center of Oregon's defense, where reads, angles, and tackling discipline matter on every snap.

Bay Area Edge

From Oakley and San Francisco roots to Oklahoma, Oregon, Cincinnati, and Houston, their paths show how much football talent can come from one family line.

Shared Football Timeline

  1. 2017 Joe enters the NFL as Cincinnati's second-round pick.
  2. 2021 Joe earns Pro Bowl recognition and helps power Cincinnati's AFC title run.
  3. 2023 Jerry begins his Oregon career and appears in eight games as a true freshman.
  4. 2024 Joe posts a 1,000-yard Houston season while Jerry continues building at Oregon.
  5. 2026 Jerry is positioned for senior leadership; Joe remains a decorated veteran back.
